Research Overview

Our research focuses on understanding and engineering nanoscale materials under extreme conditions using ion-beam physics, advanced electron microscopy, synchrotron radiation techniques, and optical characterization. We investigate structure–property relationships in metallic nanocomposites, high entropy alloys, and plasmonic systems relevant to energy and radiation environments.



Ion Beam Modification of Materials

We study defect engineering and microstructural evolution induced by low-energy and swift heavy ion irradiation. Our investigations include nanoparticle shaping, radiation damage profiling in single crystals (4H-SiC), thermal spike simulations, and ion irradiation assisted phase transformations. Structural and compositional changes are correlated using advanced microscopy and synchrotron-based techniques.



Embedded Metallic Nanoparticles & Thermal Evolution

We investigate ultra-small metallic nanoparticles (Ag, Au, AuAg, AgPt) embedded in dielectric matrices such as silica. Our work focuses on temperature-dependent growth mechanisms, alloy formation, miscibility gap studies, and in-situ heating transmission electron microscopy. Synchrotron GISAXS/GIWAXS experiments provide insight into nanoscale structural evolution.



Plasmonic and Optical Nanocomposites

We explore optical properties of metal–dielectric nanocomposites with emphasis on surface plasmon resonance tuning, particle size effects, optical absorption, photoluminescence, and nonlinear optical behavior. Our research combines experimental spectroscopy and computational modelling to understand nano-antenna physics and light–matter interactions.



High Entropy Alloys and Extreme Materials

We study multi-component alloy systems designed for structural stability, radiation tolerance, and catalytic applications. Research includes configurational entropy stabilization, ion irradiation resistance, and microstructural evolution under extreme conditions. These materials have relevance for nuclear, aerospace, and energy technologies.
