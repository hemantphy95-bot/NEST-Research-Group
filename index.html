About the NEST Research Group

Welcome to the Nanomaterials for Energy and Sustainable Technologies (NEST) Research Group. Our research focuses on the synthesis, modification, and structural evolution of advanced nanomaterials, functional materials, and complex alloy systems for energy and extreme environment applications. We aim to understand how materials respond to irradiation, high temperature, and chemically demanding conditions, and how their properties can be deliberately engineered at the nanoscale to achieve targeted functionality.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Hemant Jatav

Assistant Professor, School of Physics
Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), Indore

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist working at the interface of ion-beam physics, nanomaterials synthesis, and advanced structural characterization. My academic training includes a B.Sc. from Sri Venkateswara College, University of Delhi, an M.Sc. in Physics from IIT Delhi, and a Ph.D. from the Inter-University Accelerator Centre (IUAC), New Delhi (affiliated with JNU).

During my doctoral research, I investigated silica-embedded plasmonic alloy nanoparticles (AgAu and AgPt systems), focusing on their nucleation, growth mechanisms, thermal evolution, and ion beam-induced structural transformations. This work combined advanced microscopy with synchrotron-based scattering techniques to establish structure–property relationships in confined nanoscale functional systems.
