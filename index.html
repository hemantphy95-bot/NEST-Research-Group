About the NEST Research Group

The Nanomaterials for Energy and Sustainable Technologies (NEST) Research Group focuses on the synthesis, modification, and structural evolution of advanced nanomaterials, functional materials, and complex alloy systems. Our work aims to understand how materials behave under irradiation, high temperature, and chemically demanding conditions, and how their properties can be engineered at the nanoscale for targeted energy applications.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Hemant Jatav

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist working at the interface of ion-beam physics, nanomaterials synthesis, and advanced structural characterization. I currently serve as Assistant Professor at the School of Physics, Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), Indore.

My research integrates controlled synthesis (chemical reduction, green synthesis, sputtering, and liquid phase laser ablation), ion-beam modification using swift heavy ions and low-energy ions, and advanced characterization techniques including TEM, GISAXS/GIWAXS (DESY P03), XPS, and UV–Visible spectroscopy.

I am particularly interested in radiation-tolerant functional materials, high entropy alloys, plasmonic nanostructures, and complex alloy systems designed for energy and extreme environment applications.

Current Research Directions

Nanomaterials Synthesis

Development of metallic nanoparticles, alloy systems, thin films, and nanocomposites using chemical reduction, green synthesis, sputtering, and laser ablation techniques.

Ion Beam Engineering

Defect engineering and structural tuning using swift heavy ion and low-energy ion irradiation for controlled modification of functional materials.

Alloys for Extreme Environments

Exploration of vanadium-based and aluminum-based alloys, high entropy alloy thin films and nanoparticles, and radiation-tolerant systems for nuclear applications.

Plasmonic & Functional Materials

Optical response, catalytic behavior, and nano-antenna physics of embedded metallic nanoparticles and complex nanostructures for energy conversion technologies.

Research Vision

Our long-term vision is to design structurally complex and irradiation-tolerant functional materials platforms capable of operating reliably under extreme thermal, radiation, and chemical environments. By integrating synthesis, ion-beam modification, and advanced characterization, we aim to establish predictive structure–property relationships for next-generation sustainable energy systems.
