Welcome

Welcome to the Nanomaterials for Energy and Sustainable Technologies (NEST) Research Group. Our research focuses on ion-beam modification of nanomaterials, plasmonic systems, and advanced functional materials for energy and extreme environments.

About Me

Dr. Hemant Jatav

I am currently an Assistant Professor at the School of Physics, Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), Indore. My research lies at the intersection of ion-beam physics, nanostructured materials, and plasmonic systems.

My work focuses on defect engineering through swift heavy ion irradiation, embedded metallic nanoparticles in dielectric matrices, high entropy alloys, and optical nanocomposites. I actively employ advanced characterization techniques including TEM, synchrotron GISAXS/GIWAXS (DESY P03), XPS, and UV-Vis spectroscopy.

I am particularly interested in understanding nanoscale thermal evolution, radiation tolerance, and light–matter interaction for sustainable energy and extreme environment applications.

Research Interests

Ion irradiation induced defect engineering, embedded metallic nanoparticles, high entropy alloys, plasmon resonance engineering, synchrotron-based characterization, and optical response of nanocomposite systems.

Skills & Expertise
