About the NEST Research Group

Welcome to the Nanomaterials for Energy and Sustainable Technologies (NEST) Research Group. Our research focuses on the synthesis, modification, and structural evolution of advanced nanomaterials, functional materials, and complex alloy systems for energy and extreme environment applications. We aim to understand how materials respond to irradiation, high temperature, and chemically demanding conditions, and how their properties can be deliberately engineered at the nanoscale to achieve targeted functionality.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Hemant Jatav

Assistant Professor, School of Physics
Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), Indore

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist working at the interface of ion-beam physics, nanomaterials synthesis, and advanced structural characterization. My academic training includes a B.Sc. from Sri Venkateswara College, University of Delhi, an M.Sc. in Physics from IIT Delhi, and a Ph.D. from the Inter-University Accelerator Centre (IUAC), New Delhi (affiliated with JNU).

During my doctoral research, I investigated silica-embedded plasmonic alloy nanoparticles (AgAu and AgPt systems), focusing on their nucleation, growth mechanisms, thermal evolution, and ion beam-induced structural transformations. This work combined advanced microscopy with synchrotron-based scattering techniques to establish structure–property relationships in confined nanoscale functional systems.

Current Research Directions

Our group currently works along interconnected research pathways that integrate synthesis, irradiation, and performance evaluation of advanced functional materials.

1. Synthesis and Engineering of Nanomaterials

  • Chemical reduction methods
  • Green synthesis approaches
  • Sputtering techniques
  • Liquid phase laser ablation

These approaches enable controlled fabrication of metallic nanoparticles, alloy systems, thin films, and nanocomposites tailored for optical, catalytic, and energy-related applications.

2. Alloys and Functional Materials for Extreme Environments

  • Vanadium-based and aluminum-based alloys
  • High entropy alloy thin films and nanoparticles
  • Radiation-tolerant functional materials for nuclear environments
  • Catalytic and plasmonic nanomaterials

A central theme of our work is understanding how irradiation, thermal exposure, compositional complexity, and nanoscale confinement influence structural stability and functional performance.

Research Vision

Our long-term vision is to design and engineer next-generation functional materials platforms capable of operating reliably under extreme thermal, radiation, and chemical environments. By integrating controlled synthesis, ion-beam modification, and advanced characterization techniques such as TEM, GISAXS/GIWAXS, and XPS, we aim to establish predictive structure–property relationships that guide the development of materials for sustainable energy systems, catalysis, and nuclear technologies.

We seek to bridge fundamental materials physics with real-world technological relevance.
