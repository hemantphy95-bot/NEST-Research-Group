About the NEST Research Group

The Nanomaterials for Energy and Sustainable Technologies (NEST) Research Group focuses on the synthesis, modification, and structural evolution of advanced nanomaterials, functional materials, and complex alloy systems. Our research investigates how materials respond to irradiation, high temperature, and chemically demanding environments, and how their properties can be deliberately engineered at the nanoscale for sustainable energy applications.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Hemant Jatav

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist working at the interface of ion-beam physics, nanomaterials synthesis, and advanced functional materials research. I currently serve as Assistant Professor at the School of Physics, Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV), Indore.

My research integrates controlled synthesis routes including chemical reduction, green synthesis approaches, sputtering, and liquid phase laser ablation, with ion-beam modification using swift heavy ions and low-energy ions. These approaches allow systematic tuning of nanoscale structure and functionality.

Our group explores radiation-tolerant functional materials, vanadium-based and aluminum-based alloys, high entropy alloy thin films and nanoparticles, and plasmonic nanostructures for catalytic and energy-related applications.

Current Research Directions

Nanomaterials Synthesis

Development of metallic nanoparticles, alloy systems, thin films and nanocomposites using chemical and physical methods.

Ion Beam Engineering

Defect engineering and microstructural tuning of functional materials through swift heavy ion and low-energy ion irradiation.

Alloys for Extreme Environments

Investigation of vanadium-based, aluminum-based, and high entropy alloy systems for nuclear and high-temperature applications.

Plasmonic & Functional Materials

Optical response, catalytic behavior, and nanoscale structure–property relationships in complex functional nanostructures.

Research Vision

Our long-term vision is to design structurally complex and irradiation-tolerant functional materials capable of operating reliably under extreme thermal, radiation, and chemical conditions. By integrating synthesis, ion-beam modification, and advanced characterization techniques, we aim to establish predictive structure–property relationships for next-generation sustainable energy systems.
